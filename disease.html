Dermatological Conditions

Comprehensive guide to common skin diseases and conditions

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

A chronic condition causing itchy, inflamed skin, often triggered by irritants and allergens.

Psoriasis

Thick, scaly patches on the skin caused by an overactive immune system. Often affects elbows, knees, and scalp.

Acne

A common condition leading to pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads due to clogged pores.

Skin Rash

Changes in skin appearance, such as redness, itching, and bumps, caused by infections, allergies, or irritants.

Rosacea

A chronic condition causing redness and visible blood vessels on the face, often mistaken for acne.

Hives (Urticaria)

Raised, itchy welts on the skin caused by allergic reactions or irritants, appearing suddenly and disappearing quickly.

Contact Dermatitis

An inflammatory reaction on the skin caused by direct contact with allergens or irritants, such as chemicals or plants.

Shingles (Herpes Zoster)

A painful rash caused by the varicella-zoster virus (chickenpox virus), usually occurring in older adults.

Athlete's Foot

A fungal infection affecting the skin on the feet, causing itching, redness, and peeling between the toes.

Vitiligo

A condition in which the skin loses its pigment cells, leading to white patches on different parts of the body.

Ringworm (Tinea)

A contagious fungal infection forming a ring-like rash on the skin, typically affecting the body, scalp, or feet.

Impetigo

A bacterial skin infection causing red sores that burst and form honey-colored crusts, commonly seen in children.

Scabies

A highly contagious skin condition caused by tiny mites that burrow into the skin, resulting in intense itching and a pimple-like rash.

Melanoma

The most dangerous type of skin cancer, often appearing as a mole that changes in color, shape, or size.

Cold Sores (Herpes Simplex)

Small, painful blisters around the lips or mouth caused by the herpes simplex virus.
